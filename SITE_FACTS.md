## Services Offered

### 1. Hospice Billing
- File all NOEs (Notice of Elections), NOCs (Notice of Change), and NOTRs (Notice of Termination/Revocation)
- Create and submit claims for all payers: Medicare, Medicaid, VA, and commercial insurance
- Electronic and manual payment posting (835 file compatibility) with reconciliation and AR reporting
- Proactive claims follow-up including disputes, appeals, and reconsiderations
- Hospice billing compliance oversight and audit protection
- Live reporting of billed and unbilled accounts receivable integrated with EMR
- URL: `/hospice-billing/`

### 2. Home Health Billing
- File all NOAs (Notice of Admission)
- Create and submit claims for all payers: Medicare, Medicaid, MA, VA, and commercial insurance
- Electronic and manual payment posting with thorough reconciliation and reporting
- Proactive claims follow-up including disputes, appeals, and reconsiderations
- Federal and state billing compliance oversight
- On-demand detailed AR reporting that integrates with EMR
- URL: `/home-health-billing/`

### 3. Palliative Care Billing
- Create and submit claims for all payers: Medicare, Medicaid, MA, VA, and commercial insurance
- Create and submit secondary payer claims and patient statements
- Electronic and manual payment posting with reconciliation and AR reporting
- Proactive claims follow-up including disputes, appeals, and reconsiderations
- URL: `/palliative-care-billing/`

### 4. Authorization and Eligibility
- Patient eligibility verification: coverage, limits, deductibles, and co-pays
- Prior authorization support and submission
- Complete authorization lifecycle management (initial approvals through re-authorizations)
- Authorization appeals management for denials and eligibility disputes
- URL: `/authorization-and-eligibility/`

### 5. Contracting and Credentialing
- Contract negotiation assistance with insurance payers
- Credentialing from initial application through ongoing maintenance
- Provider enrollment and application management
- Payer relations liaison services
- URL: `/contracting-and-credentialing/`

### 6. Coding and OASIS Review (via RCO acquisition)
- Level 1: ICD-10 coding per CMS conventions, PDGM-aligned primary diagnosis selection, documentation review, active comorbidity coding
- Level 2: Coding plus assessment accuracy validation, documentation justification, assessment holds for missing face-to-face documentation
- Level 3: Coding plus assessment plus Plan of Care alignment verification, documentation gap identification, homebound status validation
- Level 4: Comprehensive coding, assessment, plan of care review, and full Plan of Care building
- URL: `/coding-and-oasis-review/`

### 7. Regulatory Resources
- Important links to Medicare Administrative Contractors (Palmetto GBA, CGS, National Government Services)
- Medicare Benefit Policy Chapters 9 and 11
- CMS Regulations and Guidance Manuals
- Hospice Medicare Billing Codes Sheet
- Hospice Payment Rates Calculator

- URL: `/regulatory-resources/`

## Process (How It Works)

| Phase | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding | EMR Immersion | Team conducts a deep dive into the client's EMR system, accelerating for familiar platforms or rapidly building proficiency with new ones. |
| Daily | Proactive Monitoring | Run daily 60-day payer reports, check overnight patient status changes, and identify problems before they become issues. |
| Ongoing | Clean Claim Submission | File all required notices (NOEs, NOCs, NOTRs, NOAs), create and submit claims for all payers with clean claim verification. |
| Ongoing | Payment Posting | Post payments electronically from 835 files or manually, with thorough reconciliation and live AR reporting integrated into the client's EMR. |
| Ongoing | Claims Follow-Up | Proactively follow every claim through disputes, appeals, and reconsiderations until it is paid; document every step with real-time AR reports. |
| Ongoing | Client Communication | Immediately notify clients of coverage changes or payer issues; follow up by phone if email is unanswered; never let anything sit idle. |

## FAQ Content

**Q: What lines of care does Advanced RevCycle serve?**
Advanced RevCycle exclusively serves post-acute care agencies: hospice, home health, and palliative care. This focused specialization means the team understands every payer rule, compliance requirement, and regulatory nuance specific to these lines of care, not generalist billing situations.

**Q: Do I have to switch my EMR system to work with ARC?**
No. Advanced RevCycle works with any EMR platform your agency already uses, including Netsmart, MatrixCare, WellSky, Axxess, Homecare Homebase, Mumms, KanTime, Careficient, Complia Health, HospiceMD, and Delta Health Technologies. The team trains to your system so you never have to disrupt operations.

**Q: How does your pricing work?**
Advanced RevCycle operates on a 100% contingency basis, meaning fees are a percentage of what is collected. There are no flat monthly fees or retainers. The company only earns when your agency collects, which keeps incentives fully aligned.

**Q: What collection rate can I expect?**
ARC maintains a 99%+ collection rate and has a 100% reduction in AR track record for clients who fully transition billing to the team. In total, the company has collected over $5 billion for agencies of all sizes.

**Q: How long does the onboarding process take?**
Transition timelines vary by agency size and complexity, but ARC has completed billing takeovers in under 30 days. The team handles all claims from day one while simultaneously learning your EMR system to ensure no revenue is lost during the switch.

**Q: Can ARC support our agency as we grow into new states?**
Yes. Advanced RevCycle serves agencies of all sizes across all 50 states, U.S. territories, Puerto Rico, and Hawaii. Clients have grown from single-location startups to multi-state operations while maintaining the same dedicated billing team.

**Q: What makes ARC different from an in-house billing team or EMR-based billing?**
ARC provides all-payer expertise, multi-EMR experience, and all MAC and state-level knowledge that a single in-house biller cannot match. Unlike EMR billing modules, ARC offers dedicated human specialists who proactively follow every claim, file appeals, and communicate in real time, with no overhead costs tied to salaries, benefits, or turnover.
